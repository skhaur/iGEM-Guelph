Recruitment

iGEM Guelph is an interdisciplinary student organization divided into sub-teams including Wet Lab, Dry Lab, Software, Business, and Outreach. Positions are open to undergraduate and graduate students from all academic disciplines. Members are hired for a specific team but will collaborate with other teams through the completion of multi-disciplinary projects and by taking on additional roles such as Human Practices Representative. This is a great opportunity to hone and broaden your technical skills, work within an interdisciplinary team, and expand your professional network. Learn more about the open positions and apply below! Contact us at igem@uoguelph.ca if you have any questions.

Join our team for the upcoming season! Click the links on the titles to apply. Applications are due January 18th but will be reviewed on a rolling basis, so may close before the deadline as positions are filled.

Please note -- if you are on mobile, recruitment information may be cut off. We suggest doing this on a computer.

Human Practices

Human Practices Representatives

Human Practices Representatives ensure that their sub-team's project is safe, ethical, and impactful. They conduct research, interview stakeholders and experts, and support other activities that identify the impacts of their project. This information is used to inform decisions throughout the project's development and execution. These responsibilities are completed in addition to the responsibilities associated with their main role within the team.

Wet Lab Team

The Wet Lab Team completes the synthetic biology research central to iGEM Guelph's project. They conduct lab work required to genetically modify microorganisms to improve existing functions or perform entirely new functions. It is required that all Wet Lab Team members be available for lab work on campus from January to November. All members of the Wet Lab team are expected to complete the responsibilities outlined in the Wet Lab Member position. Additional roles with additional responsibilities are available to members who are interested in gaining valuable scientific research experience beyond lab work. Please indicate your interest in these additional roles in the application form.

Wet Lab Member

Wet Lab Members work within a collaborative laboratory environment to complete the iGEM Guelph synthetic biology project. An example of a lab session may include running a test (ex. gel electrophoresis), recording results (ex. image gel), recording findings in a lab notebook (ex. annotate gel, record if success or failure), and cleaning up (ex. putting equipment away and doing dishes). Wet Lab Members with no additional roles will mainly be involved in lab work and recording information in the lab notebook.

Dry Lab Team

The Dry Lab team encompasses the Modelling and Engineering sub-teams. The Modelling team develops mathematical and computational models to support the Wet Lab team by predicting, characterizing, and explaining the behaviour of genetic systems. Models may be mathematical or computational representations of gene expression, protein function, and cell, system, or process behaviours depending on the needs of the Wet Lab team. The Engineering team creates novel hardware to support synthetic biology, such as bioreactors, filter media, and analytical equipment.

Director of Dry Lab

The Director of Dry Lab is responsible for managing the modelling and hardware teams by managing funding and resource procurement, communications external to the Dry Lab team, and project timelines. They also assist the Modelling and Engineering Leads with project definition and team management. They should have some knowledge and understanding of modelling and engineering, but do not need to be experts in these fields. The Director of Dry Lab must excel in leadership and project management.
